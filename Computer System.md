# Introduction to Computer System

Computers are seen everywhere around us, in all spheres of life. May it be the field of education and research, social networking, e-commerce or any other, computers have now become an indispensable part of our lives. When you use a smartphone, watch something on a smart TV, withdraw money from an ATM, or even heat food in a microwave oven, you are interacting with a computer in some form. Some common examples of computers we come across in everyday life: Desktop, Laptop, Tablet, Smartphone, Smartwatch, Smart TV, Microwave Oven, Washing Machine, Refrigerator, ATM (Automated Teller Machine), TVM (Ticket Vending Machine), POS Machine (Point of Sale), Server Computers, Cloud Computers, and more.

The word **computer** comes from the Latin word _computare_, which means **"to calculate".**

Historically, the word _computer_ did not refer to a machine at all — it referred to a **human being**. Specifically, it was used for a person whose job was to perform mathematical calculations by hand. Astronomers' assistants, wartime navigators, and census workers were all called "computers" because computing — doing calculations — was literally their profession.

The word was only later applied to electronic machines that could automate those calculations far more quickly and accurately than any human could.

So in its most literal sense: **a computer is something (or someone) that computes.**

A computer is an electronic device that accepts raw data as input, processes it according to a set of instructions, and produces meaningful information as output.

**Data:** It is the term used for raw facts and figures. For example, 134, +, 9, 'Raju', 'C' are data.

**Information:** Data represented in useful and meaningful form is information. In simple words we can say that data is the raw material that is processed to give meaningful, ordered or structured information. For example, Raju is 9 years old. This is information about Raju and conveys some meaning.

The conversion of data into information is called **data processing**, and this is the core purpose of a computer.

# Components of a Computer

The computer is a combination of hardware and software. Hardware are the physical components of a computer like motherboard, central processing unit (CPU), memory devices, storage devices, input device (keyboard), output devices (monitor) etc. while software is the set of programs or instructions. Both hardware and software together make the computer system function. A computer system comes in various forms and sizes. It can vary from a high-end server to personal desktop, laptop, tablet computer, or a smartphone.
